  • Why every startup should Pair Programming

Pair programming is when two people write code collaboratively, and each one plays the role of either navigator or driver. The role can be switched at anytime so that both people can remain engaged and share ideas and vision for the ultimate code.

The navigator tells the driver what to do and how it's done while the driver follows the instructions provided by the navigator. The important point is that the driver must also understand the ideas being conveyed by the navigator so that both team members remain on the same page as they create a final product together.

The benefits of pair programming include:

  • Each team member can spot check for coding errors on the fly with higher accuracy.
  • It increases the speed and efficiency of programming by allowing a compartmentalization of tasks associated with programming.
  • It provides experience in real-world programming context as many professional jobs work in the context of teams.
  • It improves one's code reading skills because you have to read others' code through the process.
  • It increases the creativity of the final product because, as a collaborative effort, each team member brings different ideas and vision to the table.
